Late 2022 felt like one of those moments when something fundamental shifted. ChatGPT arrived and millions of people—not just developers, not just researchers—suddenly had direct access to an AI that could write, explain, code, and create. Within months, "generative AI" went from a technical term to a dinner-table phrase.

But that was just the opening act.

The AI world is now in the middle of a second, quieter revolution—one moving from AI that *responds* to AI that *acts*. From AI that waits for your next prompt to AI that sets its own objectives and gets things done. From generative AI to agentic AI.

Understanding this shift is not about keeping up with buzzwords. It is about understanding how the technology underpinning modern work, business, and creativity is fundamentally changing again.

## What Generative AI Is

Generative AI refers to systems that create original content—text, images, video, audio, code—in response to a user's input. At the heart of these systems are **large language models (LLMs)** trained on vast amounts of human-generated data. They learn the patterns, relationships, and structures of language, then use that knowledge to produce coherent, high-quality outputs in real time.

The model is simple: you give it a prompt, it gives you a response. Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, and GitHub Copilot all fit this description. They are *reactive*—they wait for instructions and then act on them. The human is always in the loop, directing each step.
